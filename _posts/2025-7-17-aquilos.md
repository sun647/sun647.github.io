# Aquilos Cryo-FIB/SEM
## Standard Operating Protocol — Cryo Lamella Preparation

---

## Keyboard Shortcuts

| Key | Action |
|-----|--------|
| `F5` | Zoom in (one window) |
| `F6` | Live scanning |
| `F9` | Auto Contrast |
| `Shift+F7` | Scan rotation 180° — verify in bottom-right window |

---

## Protocol Steps

### Step 1 — Pre-Cooling Setup

- Record chamber pressure
- Run sputter coating + GIS twice at room temperature before cooling down
- Fill liquid nitrogen into the tank
- Cool down heat exchanger
- Set N₂ gas flow rate to **180 mg/s** and record the time

---

### Step 2 — Check Chamber & System Status

- Check whether a sample is already in the chamber

---

### Step 3 — Load Grids *(after 1 hour)*

- Load the nanosoft grid — two closest dots facing **DOWN**; rotate screw pointing **DOWN** to secure
- Place the airlock lid on the workstation
- Place the transfer rod on the airlock lid with the transfer rod valve **CLOSED**
- Close the bottom of the airlock lid, press **PUMP**
- When pump button flashes quickly → open the transfer rod valve → press **VENT**
- Open the airlock lid
- Rotate the top of the transfer rod to **UNLOCK** the retrieving pin; verify it is in unlock position
- Insert the retrieving pin into the black sample holder — press all the way to bottom
- Rotate to **LOCK** the retrieving pin
- Pull the retrieving pin all the way up and rotate to lock its position
- Close the airlock lid, press **PUMP**
- When pump button flashes quickly → **CLOSE** the transfer rod valve → press **VENT**
- Detach transfer rod from airlock lid; attach to the cryoloader
- Press **PUMP** on cryoloader; when pumping finishes, open transfer rod valve **AND** cryoloader valve
- Rotate to unlock the retrieving pin; insert all the way to insert the stage
- Rotate the protruding button to **UNLOCK** the stage, then retract the retrieving pin
- Close the column valve and the transfer rod valve

---

### Step 4 — Wake Up & Beam On

- Wake up the system
- Click the SEM window to turn **Beam On**
- Set scan rotation **180** for **BOTH** beams
- After rotation, the slot should appear at the **BOTTOM** of the grid

---

### Step 5 — Grid 1: Initial Mapping

- Go to lowest magnification
- Press `F6` for live scanning
- Increase to higher magnification
- Perform coarse focus, then fine focus at **2000×**

---

### Step 6 — Focus & Z-Link

- Select **Reduced Area**
- Perform focus
- **Link Z to Forward** *(must be in live mode)*

---

### Step 7 — Create MAPS Project

- **MAPS → Create New Project**
- Name: `MAPS-Date-Sample` (e.g. `MAPS-20241106-SampleName`)
- Change location to shared drive: `\\AS-SPC\SharedData\Chen\20241106`
  - Navigate to the folder in Windows Explorer, copy the path and paste it

---

### Step 8 — Grid 1: Pre-GIS Tile Acquisition

- Go to Grid 1 mapping position → lowest magnification
- Take a snapshot
- Add tiles; rename to `Grid1-preGIS`
- Run tile acquisition
- **Repeat all steps for Grid 2**

---

### Step 9 — Import Cryo-CLEM Data

- Go to the cryo-CLEM folder
- Select file format: `Leica*`
- Only one file should appear — click it to import (**do NOT** click *Add Leica Holder*)
- Align the imported data

---

### Step 10 — Sputter Coating: Pre-GIS

- Change N₂ gas flow rate to **210 mg/s**
- Go to Grid 1 mapping position
- Sputter coating: **Setting 1 — 15 seconds**
- Sequence: **Prepare → Run → Recover**

---

### Step 11 — GIS Deposition

- **GIS → Grid 1 — 10 seconds**

> ⚠️ **Do NOT exceed 10 s.** A thick GIS layer will severely slow down lamella milling.

---

### Step 12 — Post-GIS Sputter & Gas Reset

- Run sputter coating again
- Reset N₂ gas flow rate back to **180 mg/s**

---

### Step 13 — Post-GIS Grid Image

- Grid 1 mapping: take a high-resolution image in XT-UI
- Save as: `Grid1_afterGIS-XXX.tif`

---

### Step 14 — Align LM to SEM & Mark Lamella Positions

- Align the LM (light microscopy) image to the SEM image
- Add lamella positions on the SEM image

---

### Step 15 — Set Up AutoTEM

- Open AutoTEM
- Change the project name
- **Do NOT** change the location (keep as `D:\AutoTEM Cryo\Projects`)

---

### Step 16 — Milling: Preparation & Thinning

- Start preparation in AutoTEM
- Manually adjust lamella placement
- Perform milling and thinning in a **stepwise** manner

---

### Step 17 — Fine Polishing *(Manual)*

- In AutoTEM: select the desired lamella → right-click → **Go to Lamella Position**
- Quickly click `F6` twice to acquire an FIB image of the lamella
- **File → Import → Patterns → 300 nm**; place the pattern at the lamella
- Mill at **0.1 nA for 10 s**, then stop
- Double-click `F6` to acquire a new FIB image in case lamella has moved
- Click the SEM window and press `F6` to check lamella thickness
  - A thin lamella should be completely **dark** in SEM at **5 kV**
  - The **2 kV** image should also be dark and show cellular features
- Repeat milling if lamella is not yet thin enough
- Increase tilt angle by **+0.5°** (e.g. T=16.5 when stage tilt=16°, milling angle=9°)
- Adjust brightness and contrast with pattern on the side
- Use **Image Shift Y** to slowly move lamella into the milling pattern

> ⚠️ **Thickness check:** A properly thinned lamella appears **black** at 2 kV in SEM view.

---

### Step 18 — Document the Session

- **MAPS → File → Save Screenshot As** — save grid image with lamella positions
- In XT-UI, take SEM and FIB images after fine focus (use the **finest photo** button)
- Save as: `L2_i.tif` | `L2_s_2kV.tif` | `L2_1200x_2kV.tif` *(for LM correlation)*
- Go to lowest magnification and take a full grid image with SEM, name as "GridX_afterMilling.tif"
- Open this full grid image in paint and label the lamella number based on MAPS positions, save as a new tif image in the MAPS folder

---

### Step 19 — Unload Grids

- Press the **PUMP** button on the cryoloader — system will auto-move stage near gate
- Open the column valve and the transfer rod valve
- Retrieve the stage and lock it
- Unload the grid and put into storage

---

### Step 20 — Shutdown

- Put the system to **SLEEP**

> ⚠️ **Sleep mode is required** — skipping it wastes the ion beam.

- Copy the MAPS folder to the lab server
- Remove the heat exchanger from the tank
- Reset N₂ gas flow rate to **12 mg/s**
